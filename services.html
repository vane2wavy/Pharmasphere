General Nursing Management

  • 1. Obtain specimen for culture and sensitivity
  • 2. Monitor for toxic reactions, anaphylaxis or worsening of conditions that might indicate drug resistance.
  • 3. Watch out for signs of superinfection, Black furry tongue, Creamy Vaginal Discharge, Severe perineal itching, Stomatitis , Abdominal Cramps, Diarrhea.

Health Educations on taking Antimicrobials

Take only as per physician’s order, finish the prescribed course of medication even when you feel better, Don’t save it for future use or given to others with the same type of infection

  • SUPERINFECTION: Develops when normal flora is changed where it allows growth of bacteria that are resistant to antibiotic
  • DRUG RESISTANCE: A condition where the bacteria has developed the ability to prevail over the drugs designed to eradicate them.

Ophthalmic and Otic Agents

These are drugs with their own drug classification (e.g., antibiotics, anti-inflammatories), but were separated because they are specifically intended for treatment of eye and ear diseases or disorders

Opthalmic Agents

BETA-ADRENERGIC BLOCKERS

  • INDICATION certain types of glaucoma by reducing the production of fluid in the eye which lowers the pressure in the eye.
  • SIDE/ADVERSE EFFECTS Redness of eyes or inside of eyelids, Blurred vision or other change in vision, Different size of pupils, Discoloration of the eyeballs, Eye pain.
  • NURSING MANAGEMENT Instruct the patient on proper administration techniques for ophthalmic drops, including hand hygiene, avoiding contamination of the dropper tip, and the appropriate interval between drops if multiple medications are prescribed. Monitor intraocular pressure (IOP) regularly as directed by the healthcare provider to assess the effectiveness of the beta blocker in managing glaucoma. A missed dose should be used as soon as possible. However, if you do not remember the missed dose until the next day, skip the missed dose and go back to your regular dosing schedule. Do not double doses.
  • COMMON DRUGS Timolol maleate (TIMOPTIC), Carteolol HCl (OCUPPRESS)

MIOTICS

  • INDICATION To improve the outflow of aqueous as part of the treatment of glaucoma and reduce the risk of a posteriorly luxated lens entering the anterior chamber.
  • SIDE/ADVERSE EFFECTS Nausea and Vomiting, Tenesmus, Abdominal and/or bronchial spasm, Salivation, Sweating, Lacrimation.
  • NURSING MANAGEMENT Monitor the patient's response to miotic therapy by assessing changes in IOP, visual acuity, and any adverse effects such as ocular irritation, blurred vision, or headache. Instruct the patient to report any persistent or severe side effects promptly to their healthcare provider. Monitor input and output.
  • COMMON DRUGS Pilocarpine HCl (MIOCARPINE), Acetazolamide (DIAMOX)

MYDRIATICS

Mydriatics are a type of medicine that makes the pupil of the eye dilate (open up). Mydriatics also tend to relax the focusing muscles of the eye.

  • INDICATION used during an eye examination to allow visualization of the retina and other structures deep within the eye. Used to treat inflammatory eye conditions such as iritis and cyclitis and to reduce cycloplegia (a painful paralysis of the ciliary muscle of the eye)
  • SIDE/ADVERSE EFFECTS Eye stinging,Temporary blurred vision, Dry mouth, Eye sensitivity to light
  • NURSING MANAGEMENT Sunglasses should be worn outside because it causes blurred vision and photosensitivity. When used to prepare clients for ocular surgery, make sure pupils are fully dilated by administering every 15-20 minutes for 1 hour prior to surgery.
  • COMMON DRUGS Tropicamide (TROPICACYL), Phenylephrine (MYDFRIN)

Otic Agents

CERUMENOLYTIC AGENTS

  • INDICATIONTreatment for Otitis externa, seborrheic dermatitis and eczema afecting ear.Relieves ear discomfort; dissolves ear wax
  • SIDE/ADVERSE EFFECTSEar discomfort, Dizziness, Transient loss of hearing, Local irritation
  • NURSING MANAGEMENT Monitor the patient during and after administration for any signs of discomfort, allergic reactions, or adverse effects. Perform ear irrigation using a gentle, controlled flow of warm water or saline solution, avoiding excessive pressure or force that could damage the ear canal or tympanic membrane.
  • COMMON DRUGS Triethanolamine polypeptide oleate-condensate (CERUMENEX)

EAR ANTI-INFECTIVES

  • INDICATION kill or inhibit bacterial or fungal growth of the ear. Treatment for acute otitis externa, bacterial infection, otitis media and other diseases of the ear.
  • SIDE/ADVERSE EFFECTS Temporary stinging or burning in the ear canal
  • NURSING MANAGEMENT If using ear drops, warm the medication to room temperature by rolling the bottle between the hands before administration to reduce discomfort from cold drops. Advise the patient to avoid inserting objects (such as cotton swabs or fingers) into the ear canal, as this can push debris deeper or cause further irritation or injury.
  • COMMON DRUGS Chloramphenicol (CHLOROMYECETIN), Boric Acid (AURO-DRI/ EAR, EAR DRY)

Anti-Inflammatory Drugs

Anti-inflammatory agents are medications that reduce inflammation, swelling, and pain in the body. They are commonly used to treat conditions such as arthritis, muscle strains, and certain autoimmune disorders

CORTICOSTEROIDS

Corticosteroids are normally released by the adrenal glands. This medication mimics the action of steroid hormones and is normally involved in stress response, immune function, regulation of inflammation, carbohydrate metabolism, protein catabolism, blood volume & electrolyte levels, and behaviour. It is most famous for its anti-inflammatory effects.

INDICATION

Long term management for chronic inflammatory diseases like rheumatoid arthritis, asthma, systemic lupus erythematosus. Short term management for acute dermatitis, bronchitis and inflammation associated with infectious diseases. Traumatic injuries like head trauma and spinal cord injuries to reduce the extent of paralysis. Suppression of inflammatory responses during organ transplantation.

SIDE/ ADVERSE EFFECTS

Skin thinning or easy bruising, Osteoporosis, Long term weight gain, Oral candidiasis, Na (sodium) and glucose levels increases, Edema

Trivia:Most corticosteroids end with “solone” or “sone”.

Nursing Management

Gradually decrease the amount of corticosteroid taken by the patient. Sudden discontinuation can lead to addisonian state (decreased BP with severe cyanosis, tachypnea, tachycardia). Monitor for steroid withdrawal: fever, tiredness, apathy, lassitude and diffuse musculoskeletal pain which are life threatening.

COMMON CORTICOSTEROIDS

Dexamethasone, Prednisone, Hydrocortisone, Beclomethasone, Fluorometholon


Non-steroidal anti-inflammatory drugs (NSAIDs)

NSAIDs work by blocking prostaglandins and have analgesic, anti-inflammatory, antipyretic and even antiplatelet aggregation/ anticoagulant. It is the most widely used non-narcotic agent available over the counter (OTC).

INDICATION

Treatment of mild to moderate pain, fever, inflammatory diseases and rheumatic fever. Help reduce inflammation of blood clots after invasive procedures and those with clotting disorders like DVT and Arteriosclerosis.

COMMON SIDE EFFECTS: NSAIDS

Nausea, Skin rashes, Acid reflux, Indigestion, Distress in the epigastrium

NURSING MANAGEMENT

ASA/ Aspirin can increase the occurrence of bleeding. Stop usage 7 days prior to any surgical procedure. Remind patients to not take alcohol while taking propionic acids as it can cause hang-over like symptoms effects, hypotension and vomiting. Do not exceed 3.2g daily. Monitor for bleeding and hearing loss for patients taking indomethacin.

COMMON NSAIDs

Acetic Acid, Carboxylic Acid (salicylates), Enolic Acid, Fenamic Acid, Nonacidic Acid, Propionic Acids


COX-2 INHIBITOR

COX-2 inhibitors inhibit the cyclo-oxygenase-2 enzyme that is responsible for the inflammatory responses of the body. It has the same effects as NSAIDs but it doesn’t have anticoagulant properties. It is the World's most widely used anti-inflammatory drug

SIDE/ ADVERSE EFFECTS

Fatigue, Dizziness, Edema of left extremity, Hypertension, Dyspepsia, Nausea, Heartburn

Nursing Management

Contraindicated for patients with sulfonamides allergy.

COMMON COX2-INHIBITORS

Celecoxib, Etoricoxib, Rofecoxib


SKELETAL MUSCLE RELAXANTS/ SPASMOLYTICS

Originally, spasmolytics were used by natives in Latin and South America to poison their arrows in order to cause death by skeletal muscle paralysis. At the moment, it is used in the management of spasticity linked with severe chronic disorders such as multiple sclerosis, cerebral palsy and rheumatic disorders.

COMMON SPASMOLYTICS

Baclofen, Diazepam, Dantrolene, Botulinum Toxin Type B, Rocuronium bromide, Pancuronium bromide


ANTI-GOUT

Anti-gout drugs relieve symptoms by reducing uric acid levels.

Nursing Management

Assess clients functional mobility before starting therapy. Encouraged to avoid purine rich food such as seafood, red meat and legumes. Do not give colchicine via IM/SC to avoid local irritation. Increase fluid intake to 3L/day when taking probenecid. Allopurinol must be taken with colchicine.

COMMON ANTI-GOUT

Colchicine, Probenecid, Allopurinol


Cardiovascular Drugs

Cardiovascular drugs are medications that influence the function of the heart and blood vessels. They're some of the most commonly prescribed drugs worldwide, playing a vital role in managing various heart conditions like heart failure, high blood pressure (hypertension), and abnormal heart rhythms.

ANTI-HYPERTENSIVES

Anti-hypertensive drugs are medications used to lower high blood pressure, also known as hypertension. They work by various mechanisms to reduce the force exerted by the blood against the walls of the arteries, thereby decreasing blood pressure.

ADRENERGIC BLOCKERS

Adrenergic drugs work by increasing smooth muscle contraction and myocardial blood flow and simultaneously decreases vascular resistance which collectively increases cardiac workload. These drugs decrease blood pressure and heart rate by making the heart pump efficiently.

  • INDICATIONHypertension, Prophylaxis for migraine headaches, Treatment of severe dysmenorrhea and glaucoma.
  • SIDE EFFECTS RDry mouth, Drowsiness, Constipation, Insomnia, Nausea, Rash, Orthostatic hypotension.
  • NURSING MANAGEMENT Monitor blood pressure. Instruct clients to avoid sudden change of position. Instruct to eat sugarless candy. Instruct patients about lifestyle modifications and to avoid alcohol and strenuous activities.
  • COMMON DRUGS Clonidine, Reserpine, Methyldopa

Angiotensin-converting enzyme (ACE) inhibitors

ACE inhibitors work by blocking the action of the angiotensin-converting enzyme, which is responsible for converting angiotensin I into angiotensin II. By inhibiting this enzyme, ACE inhibitors reduce the production of angiotensin II, resulting in vasodilation (widening of blood vessels) and decreased blood pressure.

  • CONTRAINDICATION Expecting mothers, Patients with angioedema, Patients with hypersensitivity to these drugs.
  • SIDE/ADVERSE EFFECTS Fatigue, Dizziness, Mood changes, Headache, Dry non productive cough, Hyperkalemia, Angioedema, Orthostatic hypotension, Hypotension
  • NURSING MANAGEMENT Monitor blood pressure. Change position slowly. Monitor potassium levels (normal: 3.5 to 5 mEq/L). Instruct patients to avoid foods, substances, or supplements containing potassium to prevent the risk of hyperkalemia.
  • COMMON DRUGS Captopril, Enalapril, Ramipril, Quinapril, Moexipril

Angiotensin II receptor blockers (ARBs)

ARBs work by blocking the action of angiotensin II at its receptors, preventing its effects on blood vessels, and leading to vasodilation and reduced blood pressure. ARBs are more potent than other anti-hypertensive drugs and more tolerated than ACE inhibitors. It is the first line drug of choice for hypertension according to JNC 8.

  • SIDE EFFECTS Dizziness, Hypotension, Hyperkalemia, GI upset
  • COMMON DRUGS Losartan, Valsartan, Telmisartan, Candesartan, Olmesartan, Irbesartan, Eprosartan

Beta-blockers (ARBs)

Beta-blockers, also known as beta-adrenergic blockers, work by blocking catecholamines from binding to beta receptors in the hearts (beta 1) and lungs (beta 2)leading to decreased heart rate, decreased blood pressure, and reduced myocardial oxygen demand.

  • INDICATION Hypertension, Angina, Myocardial infarction
  • CONTRAINDICATION Diabetes mellitus, Asthma, Sinus bradycardia, Heart blocks
  • SIDE EFFECTS Dizziness, Lightheadedness, Drowsiness, Blurred vision, Cold hands and feet
  • NURSING MANAGEMENTObtain vital signs. Let patients take these drugs with food if in oral preparation. Observe patients for side effects and advise them to dress warmly. Lessen or taper the dose gradually instead of abrupt discontinuation of medication to avoid rebound.
  • COMMON DRUGS Metoprolol, Carvedilol, Propranolol

Calcium channel blockers (CCBs)

Calcium channel blockers work by blocking the influx of calcium ions into cardiac and smooth muscle cells. By blocking calcium channels, these drugs inhibit muscle contraction and promote vasodilation, leading to decreased blood pressure and improved blood flow to the heart. They are the first line drug of choice for atrial fibrillation and atrial flutter.

  • INDICATION Hypertension, Angina, Dysrhythmias
  • CONTRAINDICATION Cardiomyopathy
  • SIDE EFFECTS Constipation, Abnormalities in the electrical conduction system of the heart (heart blocks), Bradycardia, Low blood pressure (hypotension), Orthostatic hypotension, Kidney impairment (rare), Edema (peripheral), Reflex tachycardia (increased heart rate as a compensatory response)
  • NURSING MANAGEMENTPatients should be monitored including their blood pressure, ECG, hepatic and renal function. Instruct patients not to take these drugs with grapefruit or grapefruit juice because they can reduce the liver’s ability to eliminate CCBs from the body. Never abruptly stop the medication. Encourage patients to take the drug with meals and milk. Should not be administered with beta blockers.
  • COMMON DRUGS Nifedipine, Amlodipine, Nicardipine
